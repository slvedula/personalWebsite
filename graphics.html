MY JOURNEY

From 9th grade on...

My graphic design journey started in 9th grade when I took a graphic design class in second semester. I didn't know what to expect but what I found in the dark cave of the design room was a love for graphics. It is perfect combination of art and technical skills and it is so relevant in real-life application. Although I didn't have room to include graphics in my schedules in more recent grades, I continued to build upon my skill by participating in the Fulton County Technology Competition in 10th and 11th grade. To further increase my experience in graphic design, I studied Digital Media and Digital Marketing in my two semesters of Directed Study in 12th grade. Both courses taught me how to increase my design skills and apply them to multiple mediums including YouTube, Etsy, and Instagram.
Now, I am the creative director of Georgia Tech's Startup Exchange club, an organization that is dedicated to empowering student entreprenuership. I create flyers for every event, as well as work on the Branding of the club.
